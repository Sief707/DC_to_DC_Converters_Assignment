\# Conclusion



An open-loop buck converter was successfully designed and simulated using MATLAB/Simulink and Simscape Electrical Specialized Power Systems.



The converter achieved:

\- reduction of the input voltage from 24 V to approximately 7 V,

\- stable PWM switching operation,

\- and acceptable output voltage ripple performance.



The converter was analyzed under:

\- continuous conduction mode (CCM),

\- critical conduction mode,

\- and discontinuous conduction mode (DCM).



The transition between operating modes was successfully demonstrated by:

\- changing inductance values,

\- and varying load resistance.



The obtained waveforms verified the expected theoretical behavior of the buck converter under different operating conditions.



The project also demonstrated:

\- the influence of inductance on current ripple,

\- the effect of load variation on converter operation,

\- and the relationship between converter parameters and conduction mode behavior.



The simulation results were consistent with theoretical converter analysis and satisfied the assignment requirements.
