\# Introduction



DC-DC converters are widely used in power electronics applications to efficiently convert electrical energy between different voltage levels.



Among these converters, the buck converter is one of the most commonly used topologies for step-down voltage conversion.



The objective of this project is to design and simulate an open-loop buck converter capable of converting:

\- 24 V input voltage

to:

\- approximately 7 V output voltage

with:

\- 3 A output current.



The converter was implemented and analyzed using MATLAB/Simulink with Simscape Electrical Specialized Power Systems.



The project investigates converter operation under:

\- continuous conduction mode (CCM),

\- critical conduction mode,

\- and discontinuous conduction mode (DCM).



The effects of:

\- inductance variation,

\- and load resistance variation

on converter operation were also analyzed using simulated waveforms.



The simulation environment was configured using discrete-time analysis with a sampling time of 10e-6 sec according to assignment requirements.



The obtained results successfully demonstrated:

\- buck converter voltage regulation,

\- PWM switching operation,

\- mode transition behavior,

\- and waveform variation under different operating conditions.
